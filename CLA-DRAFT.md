# Contributor Licence Agreement — DRAFT (not in force)

DRAFT. This CLA is not in force. Contributions to vibe today are accepted under the current MIT licence and require nothing extra to be signed. This file is a placeholder for the contributor agreement that would accompany a planned relicence to AGPL-3.0 (see LICENSE-AGPL3-DRAFT). Don't treat anything below as a binding agreement yet – it's a draft for review, and the final text should be checked by a solicitor before it goes live.

## Why a CLA

vibe's core is FOSS and stays that way. andeye Ltd needs to hold enough rights in contributed code to do two things: relicence the project (the MIT to AGPL-3.0 move, and any future consistency changes across the andeye family) without chasing every past contributor, and offer paid add-ons alongside the free core. The CLA grants those rights while leaving you full ownership of your own work.

## Draft terms (individual contributor)

By submitting a contribution to vibe you would agree, at the point the CLA comes into force, that:

1. You license your contribution to andeye Ltd under a perpetual, worldwide, non-exclusive, royalty-free copyright licence to use, reproduce, modify, distribute, and sublicense it, including the right to relicense it under AGPL-3.0 or a later compatible licence.
2. You grant andeye Ltd and downstream recipients a patent licence covering any of your patents necessarily infringed by your contribution, on the same royalty-free terms.
3. You keep full ownership of and all other rights in your contribution – this is a licence, not an assignment. You can use your own work however else you like.
4. Your contribution is your original work, or you have the right to submit it, and to your knowledge it doesn't infringe anyone else's rights.
5. The contribution is provided as-is, without warranty.

Entity contributions (contributing on behalf of an employer) would use a matching corporate CLA – to be drafted alongside this one.

## How it would work in practice

Sign-off would ride on the commit (a DCO-style `Signed-off-by:` line) plus a one-time agreement recorded against your GitHub account, checked by a bot on pull requests. Nothing to sign per-PR after the first.

Until this CLA is in force, ignore all of the above: contribute under MIT as normal.
